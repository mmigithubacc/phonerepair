## Station A — Build an ESD workstation (25 min)

Start from a bare bench with the equipment in a box.

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | Place the ESD mat and connect it to the grounding point | ☐ |
| A2 | Put the wrist strap on skin (not over sleeve), snug | ☐ |
| A3 | Clip the coiled cord to the mat's ground stud | ☐ |
| A4 | Test the strap with the strap tester — show the trainer the PASS light | ☐ |
| A5 | Explain aloud to the trainer: what path does a static charge take from your hand to earth? | ☐ |
| A6 | Explain aloud: why is there a 1 MΩ resistor in the cord? | ☐ |

## Station C — Driver matching (25 min)

On the donor phones at your bench:

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | Identify the screw head type on each phone's external screws (say it aloud, then verify with the trainer's answer card) | ☐ |
| C2 | Select the correct bit and **fully seat** it — show the trainer before turning | ☐ |
| C3 | Remove and refit 4 external screws with zero slips or strips | ☐ |
| C4 | Place removed screws on the magnetic mat in a mapped layout and explain your map | ☐ |

**Rule reminder: if it slips, it strips — stop and re-check.**

## Station D — Safety scenario cards (25 min)

Draw 3 cards. For each, state (or act out) the correct response. Trainer checks against the answer key below.

| Card | Scenario | Correct response (trainer key) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Battery starts smoking on your bench | Move it to the battery bin / concrete floor away from people immediately; alert trainer; do not blow on it or douse with water |
| 2 | You notice the battery in a customer phone is swollen | Stop work; do not press, charge, or pry it; trainer/senior handles it; battery bin |
| 3 | Your wrist strap tester shows FAIL | Do not start work; swap the strap/cord and retest |
| 4 | A colleague passes you a hot soldering iron hand-to-hand | Refuse; irons transfer only via the holder |
| 5 | You spill isopropyl alcohol on the bench | Wipe immediately; no heat gun or iron use until dry; ventilate |
| 6 | A screw won't turn and the bit is starting to slip | Stop; verify bit type/size; press down firmly; if still stuck, ask trainer — never force |
| 7 | You find a screw left over after reassembly | Reopen and find its hole — an unmapped screw means the map failed; never "close it anyway" |
| 8 | Customer's phone smells of burning when plugged in | Unplug immediately; do not power on; quarantine for diagnosis |
