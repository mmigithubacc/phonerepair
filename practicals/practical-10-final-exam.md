## Station 1 — Full repair job (40 pts, ~2 h)

Treat the examiner as your customer. You must:
1. Complete the intake form (device, IMEI, fault description, **pre-existing damage
   photographed and noted**).
2. State your diagnosis and quote before opening the phone.
3. Perform the repair by the standard procedure (guide lookup allowed — using the
   model-specific guide is professional, not cheating).
4. Run the full test protocol **before final sealing** and record every result.
5. Complete the QC checklist and hand the phone back with a warranty statement.

## Station 2 — Diagnosis under time (20 pts, 30 min)

You receive a mystery-fault phone. Reach a diagnosis and justify it with documented
evidence (readings, observations, substitution tests). A correct *method* with
recorded readings scores even if time runs out before the fix.

## Station 3 — Soldering task (15 pts, ~30 min)

At the soldering bench: one wire splice (pull-tested) and through-hole joints on the
practice board, inspected under magnification. Safety gate applies (glasses, extraction,
iron in holder).

## Station 4 — Safety & knowledge spot-check (15 pts, ongoing)

During your work the examiner asks 5 random questions (safety rules, procedure
reasoning, ethics scenarios). Answer while you work — a real shop has interruptions.

## Station 5 — Professionalism (10 pts, ongoing)

Documentation quality, bench discipline throughout, and end-of-exam cleanup.

## Critical auto-fail violations

- Prying at or under a battery with a metal tool
- Working on internals with the battery connected after one warning
- Unsafe soldering iron handling (out of holder, passed by hand)
- Faking or skipping test results on the report
- Any activation-lock/FRP bypass attempt or agreement to one in the ethics questions

**Good luck. Work like every phone is a customer's phone — because from next week, it is.**
