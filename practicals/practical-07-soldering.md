# Practical 7 — Soldering Skills Lab

**Time:** 4 hours (6 core exercises + optional stretch + grading/cleanup)
**Work in pairs** at one station, but **every student solders every exercise** on their own board/wires.

## E1 — Tip prep & tinning the iron (20 min)

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| E1.1 | Set temperature ~320 °C (leaded practice solder); wait for ready light | ☐ |
| E1.2 | Wipe tip on brass wool; feed a little solder onto the tip so it coats shiny | ☐ |
| E1.3 | Show the trainer: bright, silvery, fully wetted tip — no black oxidation | ☐ |
| E1.4 | Explain aloud: why does a dry, grey tip refuse to transfer heat? | ☐ |

**Inspection criteria: tip uniformly bright and wetted; re-tin before returning it to the holder every time.**

## E2 — Tin 5 stranded wire ends (25 min)

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| E2.1 | Strip ~5 mm, twist strands tight | ☐ |
| E2.2 | Heat the wire with the iron, feed solder INTO THE WIRE, not the tip | ☐ |
| E2.3 | Repeat for 5 ends; lay them out numbered for inspection | ☐ |

**Inspection criteria: all 5 shiny and fully wicked through the strands — no blobs, no loose whiskers, insulation not melted back.**

## E3 — Splice 3 wire pairs (35 min)

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| E3.1 | Slide heatshrink onto the wire FIRST (the classic mistake — say it aloud) | ☐ |
| E3.2 | Western-union/lineman splice: hook, wrap, solder through | ☐ |
| E3.3 | Shrink the tubing over the joint (heat gun low, or iron barrel nearby — not the flame side of anything) | ☐ |
| E3.4 | Repeat for 3 pairs, then PULL-TEST each: firm steady pull, joint must not give | ☐ |

**Inspection criteria: solder flowed through the wrap; heatshrink centred and snug; 3/3 survive the pull-test.**

## E4 — Through-hole practice board, 20 joints (45 min)

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| E4.1 | Insert resistors/headers into the practice board, bend leads slightly to hold | ☐ |
| E4.2 | Iron touches PAD AND LEAD together (~1 s), feed solder to the joint (~2 s), remove solder then iron | ☐ |
| E4.3 | Complete 20 joints; trim leads above the fillet | ☐ |
| E4.4 | Self-inspect all 20 under magnification, mark any you'd redo, then hand to trainer | ☐ |

**Inspection criteria: cone-shaped shiny fillet ("volcano") wetted to pad and lead; no balls, no gaps, no cold dull-grey joints. Target ≥ 16/20 good.**

## E5 — Desolder 5 through-hole joints with wick (35 min)

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| E5.1 | Add flux to the joint (wick works poorly dry) | ☐ |
| E5.2 | Wick between iron and joint; press flat; watch solder flow into the braid | ☐ |
| E5.3 | Move to fresh braid each pass; NEVER pull the component while solder is solid | ☐ |
| E5.4 | Remove 5 components; clean residue with IPA | ☐ |

**Inspection criteria: 5 components out, pads and traces intact — no lifted pads, no scorched mask.**

## E6 — Drag-solder a pin header row (35 min)

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| E6.1 | Tack two corner pins to fix the header straight | ☐ |
| E6.2 | Generous flux along the row | ☐ |
| E6.3 | Load the tip with a bead of solder and drag slowly along the pins | ☐ |
| E6.4 | Inspect for bridges; fix any with flux + clean wick — bridges fixed properly count as pass | ☐ |

**Inspection criteria: every pin wetted, ZERO bridges remaining after rework, header straight.**

## E7 — Stretch (optional) — two-pad SMD resistor (time permitting)

| Step | Task | Done ✔ |
|---|---|---|
| E7.1 | Tin ONE pad; place the resistor with tweezers while reflowing that pad | ☐ |
| E7.2 | Solder the second pad, then reflow the first cleanly | ☐ |

**Inspection criteria: component flat and centred, both ends wetted, no tombstoning. Bonus, not graded.**
