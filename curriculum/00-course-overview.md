# Course Overview — Cellphone Repair Technician Training

## Goal
Graduates can safely diagnose and repair the most common smartphone faults —
screens, batteries, charging ports, buttons, water damage, and basic software
issues — to a professional standard, and understand how to run a small repair
business.

## Audience & prerequisites
- Beginners with no electronics background (Module 1 starts from zero).
- Basic literacy and ability to use a smartphone.
- Class size: recommended 8–12 students, max 2 students per workbench.

## Duration
- **60 hours total**: ~18 h classroom theory + ~30 h supervised practical + 12 h assessment & review.
- Standard schedule: 6 weeks, 2 sessions/week, 5 h per session.
- Intensive option: 10 consecutive working days, 6 h per day.

## Learning outcomes
By the end of the course, students can:
1. Maintain a safe, ESD-protected workspace and handle lithium batteries safely.
2. Identify every major smartphone component and explain its function.
3. Fully disassemble and reassemble common Android phones and iPhones without damage.
4. Replace screens (glued and frame-mounted), batteries, charging ports, speakers,
   cameras, and buttons.
5. Perform basic through-hole and surface soldering; understand when a board-level
   repair should be referred to a micro-soldering specialist.
6. Treat water-damaged phones using teardown, cleaning, and corrosion removal.
7. Diagnose software faults; perform factory resets, OS reflash/recovery, and backups.
8. Follow a systematic diagnostic workflow from customer intake to verified repair.
9. Price repairs, source quality parts, manage customer expectations, and keep records.

## Assessment & certification
- **Module quizzes** (10 × 10 questions) — 20% of final grade (`assessments/module-quizzes.md`)
- **Practical lab performance** — rubrics in each practical worksheet — 30%
- **Final written exam** — 20% (`assessments/final-written-exam.md`)
- **Final practical exam** — 30% (`assessments/final-practical-exam.md`)
- Pass mark: 70% overall AND pass the final practical.
- Issue certificates using `assessments/certification-checklist.md`.

## Equipment per workbench (2 students)
See `trainer-guide.md` for the full workshop list. Minimum per bench:
ESD mat + 2 wrist straps, precision screwdriver kit (Phillips #000/#00, Pentalobe P2/P5,
Torx T3–T6, Tri-point Y000), plastic pry tools & picks, metal spudger, suction cup,
tweezers (straight + curved, ESD-safe), heat gun or heating pad, isopropyl alcohol ≥ 99%,
adhesive strips/B-7000 glue, magnetic project mat, USB multimeter/ammeter, multimeter,
2–3 donor/practice phones (mixed Android + iPhone), spare parts per module.
